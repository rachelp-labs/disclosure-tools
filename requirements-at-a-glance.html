Requirements at a glance

Clinical trial registration & disclosure — EU/CTIS and US/ClinicalTrials.gov.

EU / EEA — CTIS
Registration
Regulation Regulation (EU) No 536/2014 (CTR)
Deadline CTA must be submitted and authorised before trial start1
Categories Cat. 1: Phase I · Cat. 2: Phase I/II integrated, Phase II, Phase III · Cat. 3: Phase III–IV integrated, Phase IV, low-interventional2
Results posting
Summary of results Within 12 months from end of trial in all Member States concerned3
Lay summary 12 months from end of trial; 6 months for paediatric trials and/or trials in a PIP context4
CSR When intended to support an MAA, due within 30 days after the MA is granted, the procedure is completed, or the MAA is withdrawn5
Request for Information
RFI response Up to 10 days (validation); up to 12 days (assessment)6
Transparency / public disclosure7
Cat. 1 Protocol, synopsis, patient-facing documents publish 30 months after end of trial
Cat. 1 paediatric / PIP Protocol, synopsis, patient-facing documents publish with final summary results when submitted in CTIS
Cat. 2 & 3 Protocol, synopsis, patient-facing documents, SmPC, ICF/PIS publish on first MSC decision on the CTA
Key watchouts
Grandfathered trials Trials authorised under EudraCT before the transition deadline remain on EudraCT only8
PIP / Art. 46 PIP trials with EU/EEA sites: register in CTIS and attach PDCO opinion. PIP/Art. 46 trials exclusively outside EEA: register in EudraCT Third Country File — not CTIS9

US — ClinicalTrials.gov
Registration
Regulation FDAAA 801 · 42 CFR Part 11 · NIH Policy (NIH-funded trials)
ACT definition FDA-regulated interventional, all phases other than Phase I, full criteria on ACT Checklist1
Deadline Within 21 days after first participant enrolment2
Phase I Generally excluded from mandatory ACT registration for drugs/biologics; voluntary registration may still be used for transparency or ICMJE publication purposes
NIH-funded Mandatory registration regardless of ACT status — no later than 21 days after first participant enrolment3
Results posting
Results deadline Within 12 months of PCD, unless a permitted delay or certification applies4
PCD defined as Date the final participant was examined or received an intervention for final collection of data for the primary outcome
AE table Required as part of results information5
Protocol / SAP Protocol and statistical analysis plan must be submitted with results information for ACT results submissions5
NIH-funded results Summary results due within 12 months of PCD3
Key watchouts
Common Rule / ICF Federally conducted or supported trials may require public posting of one IRB-approved informed consent form under the revised Common Rule

Cat. 1›Initial CTA
Initial CTA
Part I — Common Documents
Protocol: For publication. Upload the 'for publication' version first; add a 'not for publication' version only where needed for assessment. Note — Published 30 months after EoT.

IB: Not published. Upload an IB for a non-authorised IMP. For an authorised IMP, the SmPC may be used in place of an IB as applicable. Do not upload an IB in an SmPC field.

IMPD:
• Non-authorised IMP: full IMPD + IB
• Authorised IMP: Simplified IMPD + current SmPC

Note — SmPC: Not published for Category 1.
Part II — National Documents
  • Ethics Committee Opinion — not published (outcome as structured data only)
  • Insurance / Indemnity — not published
  • PI Curriculum Vitae — not published
  • ICF — Not published for Category 1. Upload only in the 'not for publication' slot.
EudraCT / Paediatric Check
Four scenarios still require EudraCT or PDCO involvement:
  • PIP trial with EU/EEA sites: Register in CTIS. Attach PDCO opinion + PIP Compliance Statement.
  • PIP/Art.46 exclusively outside EEA: Register in EudraCT Third Country File — NOT CTIS.
  • Art.46 study (MAH-sponsored): Submit to competent authority within 6 months of completion.
  • Grandfathered trial: Remains on EudraCT only.
RFI Watch — Monitor CTIS Workspace Daily
Monitor the CTIS sponsor workspace closely for RFIs and deadlines. Missing an RFI deadline can cause the application to lapse.

  • Validation RFI (days 1–10): ≤10 days to respond.
  • Assessment RFI Part I (days 1–45): 12 days. Non-response = lapses.
  • Assessment RFI Part II: 12 days per MSC RFI.

Ongoing Trial
Annual Safety Report (ASR)
Deadline: within 60 days of data lock date. Not published. Upload only in the 'not for publication' slot.
Protocol Changes
Does the change meet the CTR definition of a substantial modification (Art. 2(13))?

  • If yes: Submit as a Substantial Modification in CTIS.
  • If no: Submit as a Non-Substantial Modification. Notification only — no documents are published.

Substantial Modification
SM Application Dossier
Protocol Amendment: Upload the 'for publication' version first. Track-change / comparison material for assessment should stay in 'not for publication'.

Updated ICF: Not required for Cat. 1.

IMPD/IB: Update and resubmit if IMP status changed.
SM RFI Deadlines
SM Part I RFI (raised by RMS): 12 days to respond. Non-response = SM lapses.
SM Part II RFI (per MSC): 12 days per MSC.

Cat. 1›Safety Reporting
Safety Reporting
SUSARs → EudraVigilance ONLY
Fatal/life-threatening: 7-day deadline. All other: 15 days. DO NOT submit via CTIS.
Annual Safety Report (ASR)
Due annually. Via CTIS to RMS/MSC. NOT published.
Urgent Safety Measure (USM)
If immediate risk: notify the urgent safety measure in CTIS without delay. Then assess whether a consequential substantial modification is required.

Cat. 1›End of Trial
End of Trial
EoT Notification
Notify via CTIS within 15 days of end of trial in the Member State concerned / last Member State concerned / globally, as applicable.
Results Submission
CSR: Deadline: within 30 days after the MA is granted, the procedure is completed, or the MAA is withdrawn.

Summary of results + lay summary: Generally within 12 months after end of trial in all Member States concerned; 6 months if paediatric.
Structured Data Check
Confirm all CTIS structured data fields are free of personal data and CCI before submitting — cannot be redacted post-submission.
